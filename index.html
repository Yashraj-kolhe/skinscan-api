📷 Clinical & Photographic Precautions for Accurate AI Analysis
Precaution Category Clinical Guideline
💡 Lighting Requirements Use diffuse, even, natural daylight or a ring-light source. Avoid direct flash — it creates specular reflections that saturate the lesion and suppress melanin detail. Indoor fluorescent lighting introduces a green colour cast that degrades colour-based CNN features (Colour in ABCDE criteria).
🔍 Focal Blur Warning Maintain a minimum shooting distance of 5–8 cm with macro focus enabled. Motion blur, camera shake, or depth-of-field defocus causes MobileNetV2's high-frequency texture features (dermoscopic patterns) to degrade. Images with BRISQUE score > 50 should be recaptured before submission.
🧹 Hair Artefact Removal Body hair creates dark, elongated occluding structures that can be misinterpreted by the CNN as irregular borders (B-criterion) or colour streaks (C-criterion). Gently stretch the skin taut or use transparent surgical tape to flatten hair around the lesion before capture. Future Phase III builds will incorporate automatic DullRazor hair removal preprocessing.
📐 Framing & Centering Centre the lesion within the frame with a 10–15 mm clear margin of surrounding healthy skin visible. Cropped or edge-touching lesions prevent the model from evaluating border irregularity (B-criterion) correctly. The image will be resized to 224×224 px — extreme aspect ratios distort the lesion shape.
🔄 Multiple Angles Capture 3–5 images at slightly varying angles and select the sharpest, most evenly lit image for upload. Dermoscopic asymmetry can be direction-dependent; multiple views improve the quality of clinical corroboration alongside the AI result.
🚀 Future Enhancements — Phase III Development Roadmap
Enhancement Technical Description & Clinical Impact
🧠 Explainable AI (Grad-CAM) Gradient-weighted Class Activation Mapping (Grad-CAM) will generate a heat map overlay on the original image, highlighting the exact pixel regions that drove the malignancy classification. This transforms the model from a "black box" to an interpretable clinical aid, allowing dermatologists to verify whether the AI focused on the actual lesion morphology or artefactual regions.
🔎 Automated Blur Detection A BRISQUE (Blind/Referenceless Image Spatial Quality Evaluator) or Laplacian variance module will automatically assess image sharpness before inference is triggered. Images scoring below an acceptable quality threshold will prompt the user to recapture rather than returning potentially unreliable predictions, improving clinical safety.
🏥 eSanjeevani Integration Integration with India's national telemedicine network (eSanjeevani OPD) via FHIR-compliant API will enable direct referral workflow: a high-risk SkinScan AI result can auto-populate a telemedicine referral form and schedule a dermatologist teleconsultation, bridging rural screening to specialist care within a single session.
🎯 7-Class Classification Expanding the model output from binary (Malignant/Benign) to all 7 HAM10000 diagnostic categories (MEL, NV, BCC, AKIEC, BKL, DF, VASC) with a softmax output layer. This provides dermatologists with a differential probability distribution across lesion subtypes, significantly enhancing the clinical utility of the AI report.
📊 Longitudinal Tracking Firebase Firestore-backed per-patient scan history with timeline visualisation will allow monitoring of lesion evolution over successive scans — directly operationalising the E (Evolving) criterion of ABCDE, which is currently impossible without longitudinal data.
